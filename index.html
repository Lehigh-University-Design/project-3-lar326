Biography
“Pressure is privilege - it only comes to those who earn it.”
Billie Jean King

Billie Jean King is an American tennis player who held the #1 world tennis player position for 6 years. Starting tennis at age 11 she would go on to turn pro in 1959 at age 16. Her first international recognition was in 1961 when she became ½ of the youngest pair to ever win the Wimbledon women’s doubles title. Throughout her career Billie Jean went on to win 20 Wimbledon titles, 13 US titles, and 39 total grand slam titles.

Billie Jean Moffit was born November 22, 1943, in Long Branch California. Growing up she was competitive in multiple sports before ultimately finding tennis. She was introduced to the sport by her father, who took her to local courts to play. Billie Jean quickly developed a passion for tennis and showed remarkable talent from a young age. She began playing competitively in her early teens, laying the foundation for what would become a legendary career in the sport. She attended California State University in Los Angeles, but did not graduate, as she left school in 1964 to focus on competing in tournaments. BJK would also go on to marry Larry King in 1965.

Billie Jean has been an advocate for equality and social justice from a young age. One of her first injustices that fueled this work was at a tournament when she was younger. Billie Jean was forbidden from being a part of a group photo because she was wearing tennis shorts that her mother had made her instead of the usual white tennis dress. As her career progressed she began to advocate for equal prize money between men and women’s competitions. In 1970 at the Pacific Southwest Open the prize money for men was $12,500 and for women was $1,500. This resulted in Billie Jean and 8 other women refusing to play and began to start a women’s only tournament. In 1971 by winning the Virginia Slims Tour for women, she became the first women athlete to win over $100,000 in prize money. In 1973, the US open became the first major tournament to offer equal prize money to both sexes.

Since retiring from professional tennis, Billie Jean King has remained highly active and influential in various fields. She has dedicated herself to advocating for social justice, gender equality, and LGBTQ+ rights. King co-founded the Women's Tennis Association (WTA) and the Women's Sports Foundation, organizations that have played crucial roles in advancing women's sports and promoting equality in athletics. Additionally, Billie Jean King has been involved in various philanthropic endeavors, including initiatives aimed at promoting access to sports and education for underserved communities. She continues to be a prominent speaker and activist, using her platform to champion causes related to diversity, inclusion, and empowerment. Billie Jean King's impact extends beyond the world of tennis, demonstrating her enduring commitment to creating positive change in society.

Battle of the Sexes
“Champions keep playing until they get it right.”
Billie Jean King

In 1973, Billie Jean played Bobby Riggs in the battle of the sexes. Bobby claimed that the women’s game was so inferior that an older amateur like himself could beat the top female players.

Billie Jean beat Bobby Riggs 6–4, 6–3, 6–3 and the match is still considered one of the most significant events in developing greater recognition and respect for women's sports, especially tennis.

The match took place at the Houston Astrodome in front of 30,492 spectators and a television audience of about 50 million people in the U.S., and 90 million in 37 countries. In 1973, King became the first president of the Women's Tennis Association, a women's players union. In 1974, she and her husband Larry King helped found womenSports magazine and started the Women's Sports Foundation. Billie Jean King is a member of the Board of Honorary Trustees for the Sports Museum of America, a museum home to the Billie Jean King International Women's Sports Center, a women's sports hall of fame and exhibit.

The "Battle of the Sexes" is a 2017 sports film directed by Jonathan Dayton and Valerie Faris. The movie portrays the famous 1973 tennis match between Billie Jean King (played by Emma Stone) and Bobby Riggs (played by Steve Carell), which became a cultural phenomenon and a landmark event for gender equality in sports. The film dives into Billie Jean King's personal and professional life leading up to the match, highlighting her struggles against gender discrimination in tennis and her advocacy for equal pay and opportunities for female players. It also explores Bobby Riggs' persona and the media attention surrounding the highly publicized match. "Battle of the Sexes" received critical acclaim for its performances, especially Emma Stone's portrayal of Billie Jean King, and for its depiction of the social and political context of the time. The movie effectively captures the significance of the match and its impact on the broader conversation about gender roles and equality in sports.

World Team Tennis

World TeamTennis (WTT) is a professional tennis league co-founded by Billie Jean King in 1973. WTT features teams representing various cities across the United States. Matches are played in a team format where men's singles, women's singles, men's doubles, women's doubles, and mixed doubles are contested. The league has a fast-paced and engaging style, with unique rules such as no-ad scoring, substitutions, and coaching timeouts.

WomenSports Magazine

WomenSports was groundbreaking as one of the first magazines dedicated solely to women in sports. It featured in-depth coverage of women's athletics, including profiles of athletes, coverage of major sporting events, training tips, and articles on health and fitness specific to women athletes. The magazine played a crucial role in advocating for gender equality in sports and challenging stereotypes about women's athletic abilities. WomenSports Magazine was notable for its commitment to showcasing female athletes across a wide range of sports, from tennis and track to basketball and gymnastics. It provided a platform for athletes to share their stories and accomplishments, helping to raise awareness and recognition of women's sports.

Women's Sports Foundation

The Women's Sports Foundation (WSF) is a nonprofit organization dedicated to advancing the lives of girls and women through sports and physical activity. It was founded in 1974 by Billie Jean King, along with other influential athletes and activists, with a mission to ensure all girls and women have access to sports and opportunities for leadership and empowerment through athletics. This foundation has been instrumental in driving progress towards gender equality in sports and has been a leading voice in promoting opportunities for girls and women to participate and excel in athletics. Through their efforts, they aim to create a future where all girls and women have the chance to experience the transformative power of sports and achieve their full potential on and off the field.

BJK Enterprises

Billie Jean King Enterprises (BJK Enterprises) encompasses the various business ventures and initiatives associated with Billie Jean King. Founded by Billie Jean King, this enterprise focuses on leveraging her influence and expertise in tennis, sports, and advocacy to create positive impacts and opportunities. This program serves as a platform to extend Billie Jean King's legacy beyond her tennis career, amplifying her voice and impact in areas such as gender equality, diversity, and inclusion and aiming to inspire positive change and create opportunities for future generations in sports and beyond.
